## SESSION 1: STRICT INTERVIEWER (30 min)

### ChatGPT Setup Prompt (Copy-Paste):

```
For the next 30 minutes, act as a strict technical interviewer for a senior QA automation role.

Today's focus: COMMON PHRASES & EXPRESSIONS

Your role:
- Ask me interview questions that allow me to use professional phrases
- Interrupt me IMMEDIATELY when I use weak phrases like "I think", "I feel", "maybe", "I guess"
- Say "Stop. Instead of 'I think', say 'In my opinion' or 'I believe'. Try again."
- Don't accept casual language - push for professional phrasing
- Listen for phrases like: "In my opinion", "From my experience", "I'm confident that", "Based on what I've seen"
- Keep a professional, demanding tone
- After 30 minutes, give me:
  * Professional phrase usage rating (0-10)
  * 3 weak phrases I used ("I think", "maybe", etc.)
  * 3 professional phrases I used well
  * 1 specific phrase to practice more tomorrow

Start by introducing yourself as the Director of Engineering, then ask: "Tell me about your automation testing philosophy and approach. I want to hear your opinions backed by experience."
```

---

### Practice Topics for This Session:

1. **Testing Philosophy:** "What's your opinion on test automation?"
   - Must use: "In my opinion", "I believe that"

2. **Experience Showcase:** "Tell me about your automation achievements."
   - Must use: "In my previous role", "From my experience"

3. **Confidence in Skills:** "Can you handle building our framework from scratch?"
   - Must use: "I'm confident that", "Without a doubt"

4. **Problem Diagnosis:** "Our tests are flaky. What might be the issue?"
   - Must use: "It seems", "Perhaps", "Based on what I've seen"

5. **Strong Opinions:** "Should we aim for 100% test coverage?"
   - Must use: "I'd argue that", "From my perspective"

---

### Success Criteria:

✅ Speak at least 20 minutes out of 30
✅ Use "In my opinion" / "I believe" at least 5 times
✅ Use "From my experience" / "In my previous role" at least 3 times
✅ Use confidence phrases at least 3 times
✅ Avoid "I think", "maybe", "I guess" - use professional alternatives
