## ‚ö° TOP 5 GRAMMAR RULES

**Rule 1:** Use HOWEVER instead of "but" in professional contexts
- ‚úÖ "I prefer automation. However, manual testing is important."
- ‚úÖ "The deadline was tight. However, we delivered on time."

**Rule 2:** Use THEREFORE/CONSEQUENTLY instead of "so" for results
- ‚úÖ "Tests were slow. Therefore, I optimized them."
- ‚úÖ "The framework lacked reporting. Consequently, I integrated Allure."

**Rule 3:** Use MOREOVER/FURTHERMORE instead of "and also" to add information
- ‚úÖ "I know Playwright. Moreover, I'm experienced with Selenium."
- ‚úÖ "The framework is scalable. Furthermore, it's well-documented."

**Rule 4:** Use FIRST/THEN/FINALLY for clear sequences
- ‚úÖ "First, I analyze requirements. Then, I design tests. Finally, I automate."
- ‚úÖ "Initially, I reviewed the code. Next, I identified the issue. Finally, I fixed it."

**Rule 5:** Use ON THE OTHER HAND for balanced comparisons
- ‚úÖ "API testing is faster. On the other hand, UI testing validates user experience."
- ‚úÖ "Selenium is mature. On the other hand, Playwright offers modern features."

## ‚ùå TOP 5 MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. ‚ùå "I work here and also I study and also I learn"
   ‚úÖ "I work here. Moreover, I study. Additionally, I learn new skills."

2. ‚ùå "But I think automation is better"
   ‚úÖ "However, I believe automation is more efficient."

3. ‚ùå "So I implemented the framework. So it worked well."
   ‚úÖ "I implemented the framework. As a result, it worked well."

4. ‚ùå "Although I prefer automation, but manual testing is needed"
   ‚úÖ "Although I prefer automation, manual testing is still necessary."

5. ‚ùå "And then I did this and then I did that"
   ‚úÖ "First, I did this. Then, I did that. Finally, I completed it."
