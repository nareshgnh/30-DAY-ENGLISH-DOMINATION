## SECTION 1: CORE SKILLS

### SKILL 1: Polite Requests

**Instead of direct orders, use polite requests:**

❌ "Send me the test report"
✅ "Could you please send me the test report?"

❌ "Fix this bug"
✅ "Would you mind looking into this bug when you get a chance?"

**Key Phrases:**
- "Could you please..."
- "Would you mind..."
- "I'd appreciate if you could..."
- "When you have time, could you..."

---

### SKILL 2: Professional Disagreement

**Disagree without being disagreeable:**

❌ "That's wrong"
✅ "I see your point, however I have a different perspective"

❌ "That won't work"
✅ "I have some concerns about this approach. Could we consider an alternative?"

**Key Phrases:**
- "I see your point, however..."
- "I have a different view on this..."
- "I respectfully disagree because..."
- "Have we considered..."
- "What if we tried..."

---

### SKILL 3: Meeting Communication

**Effective meeting participation:**

**Contributing Ideas:**
- "I'd like to suggest..."
- "Building on what [name] said..."
- "To add to that point..."

**Clarifying:**
- "Just to clarify..."
- "Could you elaborate on..."
- "Let me make sure I understand correctly..."

**Summarizing:**
- "To summarize the key points..."
- "So what we've agreed is..."
- "Let me recap the action items..."

---

### SKILL 4: Giving Feedback

**Constructive, not critical:**

❌ "Your code is buggy"
✅ "I've noticed a few edge cases that might need handling. Could we review them together?"

❌ "This test is bad"
✅ "I have some suggestions to improve test stability. Would you like to discuss?"

**Sandwich Method:**
1. Positive: "I appreciate the effort on this framework..."
2. Improvement: "I think we could enhance maintainability by..."
3. Positive: "Overall, great work on getting this implemented quickly"

## SECTION 3: SCENARIOS & RESPONSES

**Scenario 1: Dev Pushes to Skip Testing**

Dev: "Can we skip testing this? It's a small change."

❌ Bad: "No, we can't skip testing"
✅ Good: "I understand it's a small change. However, from my experience, small changes can have unexpected impacts. Could we at least run smoke tests to ensure nothing breaks?"

**Scenario 2: Manager Asks for Unrealistic Timeline**

Manager: "Can you automate all 500 test cases by Friday?"

❌ Bad: "That's impossible"
✅ Good: "I appreciate the urgency. To deliver quality automation by Friday, I'd suggest we prioritize the top 50 critical flows first. Would that work? I can share a prioritized plan within an hour."

**Scenario 3: Teammate's Code Has Issues**

❌ Bad: "Your code is buggy"
✅ Good: "Hey, I noticed a couple of edge cases while testing. Could we pair up for 15 minutes to review them? I have some ideas that might help."
