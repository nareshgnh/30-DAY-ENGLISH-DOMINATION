## BREATHING EXERCISE (2 min):

**Exercise 1: Power Breathing**
- Breathe in for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Breathe out for 4 counts
- As you breathe out, say loudly: "I speak English clearly and confidently."

**Exercise 2: Fluency Breathing**
- Take a deep breath
- On the exhale, say this sentence smoothly: "I am a QA Automation Engineer with excellent communication skills."
- Repeat 3 times, getting smoother each time

**Exercise 3: Energy Breathing**
- Quick breath in
- Explosive breath out while saying: "Today I master Present Tense! Let's go!"
- Repeat 3 times with increasing energy

## WARM-UP SENTENCES (5 min):

**Read these aloud 3 times each. Focus on clarity, not speed.**

1. I work as a QA Automation Engineer at my current company.

2. I am currently learning English to prepare for senior interviews.

3. I test software applications every day to ensure quality.

4. Right now, I am building my automation framework using Playwright.

5. I usually work on test automation, but today I am focusing on improving my speaking skills.

**Why these sentences?** They use both Present Simple (habits, facts) and Present Continuous (actions happening now). You'll learn the difference in today's Learning file!

---

## TONGUE TWISTERS (3 min):

**Practice each 5 times. Start slowly, then increase speed.**

1. **"I work at work, working on the workflow."**
   (Focus on 'w' sound - common challenge for Telugu/Hindi speakers)

2. **"She tests the system systematically every single session."**
   (Focus on 's' sound and rhythm)

3. **"Peter's programming perfectly, preparing professional projects."**
   (Focus on 'p' sound and alliteration)

**Tip:** If you mess up, that's PERFECT. It means your mouth is learning new movements. Keep going!
