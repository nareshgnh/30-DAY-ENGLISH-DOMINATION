## SECTION 1: RULES & EXPLANATIONS

---

### CORE RULE 1: TIME Prepositions (in, on, at)

**Explanation:**
Time prepositions follow a pattern from GENERAL to SPECIFIC:
- **IN** = Longer periods (months, years, seasons, centuries)
- **ON** = Specific days and dates
- **AT** = Specific times and moments

**Structure:**
- IN + longer time periods
- ON + days/dates
- AT + clock times/moments

**10 Examples:**

1. **I joined TCS in 2020.**
   (Year = longer period = IN)

2. **I have a meeting on Monday.**
   (Specific day = ON)

3. **The meeting starts at 3 PM.**
   (Specific time = AT)

4. **I learned Selenium in January.**
   (Month = longer period = IN)

5. **I'm free on the 15th of March.**
   (Specific date = ON)

6. **The build runs at midnight every day.**
   (Specific time = AT)

7. **We deploy on Fridays.**
   (Specific day = ON)

8. **I worked at Wipro in 2018.**
   (Year = IN)

9. **I'll finish this task in the morning.**
   (Part of day = IN)

10. **The interview is on December 5th at 10 AM.**
    (Specific date = ON, Specific time = AT)

**Common Mistake Indians Make:**
‚ùå "I have a meeting in Monday at morning"
‚úÖ "I have a meeting on Monday in the morning" OR "...on Monday morning"

‚ùå "I joined at 2020"
‚úÖ "I joined in 2020"

**Why?** We translate directly from Hindi/Telugu where one word covers all time references. English is PRECISE.

**Memory Trick:**
**"BIG to SMALL: IN ‚Üí ON ‚Üí AT"**
Think: IN (big container like a month) ‚Üí ON (standing on a specific day) ‚Üí AT (pointing at exact time)

**Time Preposition Table:**
- IN: the morning/afternoon/evening, January, 2020, summer, the 21st century
- ON: Monday, weekends, my birthday, January 15th, the 1st of May
- AT: 3 PM, midnight, noon, night, the moment, that time

---

### CORE RULE 2: PLACE Prepositions (in, on, at)

**Explanation:**
Place prepositions also follow GENERAL to SPECIFIC:
- **IN** = Enclosed spaces (cities, countries, buildings, rooms)
- **ON** = Surfaces and floors (streets, floors, platforms)
- **AT** = Specific points and locations (addresses, venues)

**10 Examples:**

1. **I work at TCS.**
   (Specific company/location = AT)

2. **I live in Bangalore.**
   (City = enclosed space = IN)

3. **My office is on the 5th floor.**
   (Floor = surface/level = ON)

4. **I work in India.**
   (Country = large enclosed area = IN)

5. **The conference is at the Marriott Hotel.**
   (Specific venue = AT)

6. **My desk is on MG Road.**
   (Street = ON)

7. **I'm sitting in the meeting room.**
   (Enclosed room = IN)

8. **I'll meet you at the bus stop.**
   (Specific point = AT)

9. **The server is located in the data center.**
   (Enclosed building = IN)

10. **I have a poster on the wall.**
    (Surface = ON)

**Common Mistake Indians Make:**
‚ùå "I work in TCS" (when you mean the company as a location)
‚úÖ "I work at TCS" (company = specific location)
‚úÖ "I work in the TCS building" (building = enclosed space)

‚ùå "I live at Bangalore"
‚úÖ "I live in Bangalore"

**Why?** We use one preposition for all places in our languages. English distinguishes based on the type of space.

**Memory Trick:**
**"IN = INSIDE, ON = ON TOP OF, AT = AT A POINT"**
Ask yourself: Am I inside it? (IN) | Am I on its surface? (ON) | Is it a specific point/address? (AT)

**Place Preposition Guide:**
- IN: Bangalore, India, the office, the room, the building, the car
- ON: the 3rd floor, MG Road, the platform, the wall, the table
- AT: TCS, the airport, the bus stop, 123 Main Street, home

**Special Case:**
- "at home" (we say AT home, not IN home)
- "at work" (AT work, not IN work - when you mean the location)
- "in the workplace" (IN when it's the space itself)

### CORE RULE 3: Other Important Prepositions (by, for, with, about, from, to)

**Explanation:**
Each preposition has specific uses in professional context:
- **BY** = Deadline, method, agent
- **FOR** = Purpose, duration, recipient
- **WITH** = Using/accompanying, association
- **ABOUT** = Topic, approximately
- **FROM...TO** = Range, origin to destination

**10 Examples:**

1. **I'll complete this task by Friday.**
   (Deadline = BY)

2. **I use Selenium for automation testing.**
   (Purpose = FOR)

3. **I work with Python and Java.**
   (Using these tools = WITH)

4. **I have a question about the test framework.**
   (Topic = ABOUT)

5. **I worked at Wipro from 2018 to 2021.**
   (Time range = FROM...TO)

6. **This bug was fixed by the development team.**
   (Agent who did it = BY)

7. **I worked on this project for 6 months.**
   (Duration = FOR)

8. **I'm good at automation testing.**
   (Skill/ability = AT)

9. **I learned about Playwright in a workshop.**
   (Topic = ABOUT)

10. **Tests run from 9 PM to 6 AM.**
    (Time range = FROM...TO)

**Common Mistake Indians Make:**
‚ùå "I'm good in automation" OR "I'm good with automation"
‚úÖ "I'm good at automation"

‚ùå "I learned for Playwright"
‚úÖ "I learned about Playwright"

‚ùå "I'll finish until Friday"
‚úÖ "I'll finish by Friday"

**Why?** Each preposition has specific meanings. We can't substitute them randomly.

**Memory Trick:**
**"BY = DEADLINE, FOR = PURPOSE, AT = SKILL"**
- BY Friday = before Friday ends
- FOR testing = the purpose is testing
- good AT = skilled AT
- FROM...TO = range

**Common Preposition Patterns:**
- good/bad/excellent AT (skill)
- used FOR (purpose)
- work WITH (tools/people)
- talk/learn ABOUT (topic)
- finish BY (deadline)
- work FROM...TO (time range)
- responsible FOR (responsibility)
- depend ON (reliance)
- belong TO (ownership)

---

### WHY THIS MATTERS FOR INTERVIEWS:

**Interviewers notice preposition mistakes:**

‚ùå "I worked in Infosys from 2018 until 2020"
‚úÖ "I worked at Infosys from 2018 to 2020"

‚ùå "I'm good in automation and testing"
‚úÖ "I'm good at automation and testing"

‚ùå "I have meeting in Monday on 3 PM"
‚úÖ "I have a meeting on Monday at 3 PM"

**Perfect prepositions = Native-level precision = Interview confidence**

**Common interview prepositions:**
- "I worked AT [company] IN [city] FROM [year] TO [year]"
- "I'm good AT [skill]"
- "I learned ABOUT [topic] IN [time period]"
- "I'll join BY [date]"
- "I worked ON [project] FOR [duration]"

---

### WHY THIS MATTERS FOR WORKPLACE:

**Daily communication:**

**Emails:**
- "I'll send the report BY EOD"
- "Meeting ON Monday AT 3 PM IN Conference Room A"
- "I'm working ON the automation framework"

**Meetings:**
- "I joined the company IN 2020"
- "I'll finish this BY tomorrow"
- "I'm good AT debugging complex issues"

**Wrong prepositions = Unprofessional = Confusing communication**
**Right prepositions = Clear = Trustworthy colleague**
