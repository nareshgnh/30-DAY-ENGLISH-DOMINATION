## SECTION 1: WEEK 1 COMPREHENSIVE REVIEW

---

### DAY 1 RECAP: Present Tenses

**Key Rules:**
- **Present Simple:** Habits, facts, regular actions
  - "I work at TCS"
- **Present Continuous:** Actions happening now, temporary situations
  - "I am working on automation"
- **State verbs:** Use Simple, not Continuous
  - "I know Python" (NOT "I am knowing")

**Quick Test:**
1. I _____ (work/am working) at Infosys. (permanent job)
2. Right now, I _____ (test/am testing) the application.
3. I _____ (understand/am understanding) the framework.

**Answers:** 1. work, 2. am testing, 3. understand

---

### DAY 2 RECAP: Past Tenses

**Key Rules:**
- **Past Simple:** Completed actions at specific time
  - "I worked at Wipro in 2019"
- **Present Perfect:** Life experiences, duration from past to now
  - "I have worked in QA for 6 years"
- **Past Continuous:** Background actions
  - "I was testing when the bug occurred"

**Quick Test:**
1. I _____ (worked/have worked) at TCS from 2018 to 2021.
2. I _____ (worked/have worked) in QA for 6 years. (still working)
3. I _____ (tested/was testing) when you called.

**Answers:** 1. worked, 2. have worked, 3. was testing

---

### DAY 3 RECAP: Future Tenses

**Key Rules:**
- **will:** Spontaneous decisions, predictions, opinions
  - "I will help you right now"
- **going to:** Plans, evidence-based predictions
  - "I am going to learn Kubernetes next month"
- **Present Continuous for future:** Scheduled events
  - "I am meeting the manager tomorrow at 3 PM"

**Quick Test:**
1. I _____ (will help/am going to help) you right now. (instant decision)
2. I _____ (will learn/am going to learn) Docker next month. (already planned)
3. I _____ (will attend/am attending) a workshop on Monday at 2 PM. (scheduled)

**Answers:** 1. will help, 2. am going to learn, 3. am attending

---

### DAY 4 RECAP: Articles

**Key Rules:**
- **a/an:** One non-specific thing, professions
  - "I am a QA engineer"
- **the:** Specific thing both parties know
  - "I work on the automation framework" (your specific framework)
- **Zero article:** General concepts, technologies, languages
  - "I know Python" "Testing is important"

**Quick Test:**
1. I am _____ automation engineer.
2. I work on _____ automation framework at my company.
3. _____ Selenium is _____ popular tool.

**Answers:** 1. an, 2. the, 3. X/a (Selenium/a popular tool)

---

### DAY 5 RECAP: Prepositions

**Key Rules:**
- **Time:** IN (month/year), ON (day/date), AT (time)
  - "in 2020, on Monday, at 3 PM"
- **Place:** IN (city), ON (floor/street), AT (specific location)
  - "in Bangalore, on the 5th floor, at TCS"
- **Other:** good AT, deadline BY, duration FOR, work WITH
  - "good at testing, finish by Friday, work for 3 years, work with Python"

**Quick Test:**
1. I have a meeting _____ Monday _____ 3 PM.
2. I work _____ TCS _____ Bangalore.
3. I'm good _____ automation testing.

**Answers:** 1. on/at, 2. at/in, 3. at

---

### DAY 6 RECAP: Common Grammar Fixes

**Key Rules:**
- **do/does/did:** HE/SHE/IT = does | Others = do | Past = did
  - "He doesn't know" (NOT "He don't")
- **have/has/had:** HE/SHE/IT = has | Others = have | Past = had
  - "She has experience" (NOT "She have")
- **is/are/was/were:** I am, You are, He/She/It is, We/They are
  - "They were testing" (NOT "They was")

**Quick Test:**
1. He _____ (don't/doesn't) know Selenium.
2. She _____ (have/has) 6 years of experience.
3. They _____ (was/were) testing yesterday.

**Answers:** 1. doesn't, 2. has, 3. were

## SECTION 2: SENTENCE STRUCTURE FUNDAMENTALS

---

### BASIC SENTENCE STRUCTURE: S-V-O

**Rule:** English follows Subject-Verb-Object order

**Structure:**
- **Subject** (who/what does the action)
- **Verb** (the action)
- **Object** (what receives the action)

**Examples:**
1. **I** (S) **test** (V) **applications** (O).
2. **She** (S) **writes** (V) **test cases** (O).
3. **We** (S) **use** (V) **Selenium** (O).

**For Questions:**
- **Question word + Auxiliary + Subject + Verb + Object?**
- "What do you test?"
- "Where does she work?"

**For Negatives:**
- **Subject + Auxiliary + not + Verb + Object**
- "I don't test manually"
- "She doesn't work on weekends"

---

### EXPANDING SENTENCES: Adding Details

**Pattern 1: Add Time**
- Basic: "I test applications"
- With time: "I test applications every day"
- "I worked at TCS from 2018 to 2021"

**Pattern 2: Add Place**
- Basic: "I work"
- With place: "I work at Infosys in Bangalore"

**Pattern 3: Add How/Why**
- Basic: "I use Selenium"
- With how: "I use Selenium for browser automation"
- With why: "I use Selenium because it's reliable"

**Pattern 4: Combine Everything**
- "I work at TCS in Bangalore on automation projects using Python"
- Subject + Verb + Place + Preposition + Object + Preposition + Tool

---

### COMPLEX SENTENCES: Combining Ideas

**Using "and":**
- "I test applications and I write test reports"

**Using "but":**
- "I know Selenium, but I'm learning Playwright"

**Using "because":**
- "I use automation because it saves time"

**Using "when":**
- "I found the bug when I was testing the login module"

**Using "which/that":**
- "I work on the framework that handles API testing"
