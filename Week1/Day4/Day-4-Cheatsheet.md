## ‚ö° TOP 5 GRAMMAR RULES

**Rule 1:** Use "a" or "an" for professions, first mentions, and one non-specific thing
- ‚úÖ I am a QA engineer (profession)
- ‚úÖ I found a bug (first mention)
- Use "an" before vowel SOUNDS: an error, an API, an hour

**Rule 2:** Use "the" for specific things both parties know
- ‚úÖ I work on the automation framework (specific one at your company)
- ‚úÖ I found a bug. The bug was critical. (second mention)
- ‚úÖ I am the lead QA (unique in your team)

**Rule 3:** Use NO article (zero article) for general concepts, technologies, languages
- ‚úÖ I work in software testing (general field)
- ‚úÖ I know Python and Java (languages/technologies)
- ‚úÖ Automation is important (concept in general)

**Rule 4:** First mention = "a/an", Second mention = "the"
- ‚úÖ I found a bug. The bug was in the login module.
- ‚úÖ I attended a workshop. The workshop was informative.

**Rule 5:** Decision tree - Is it specific? Use "the". Is it one of many? Use "a/an". Is it general? No article.

## üìù QUICK ARTICLE FORMULAS

**"a/an" - One of Many:**
- I am a tester (one of many testers)
- I found a bug (one bug, first mention)
- I need an hour (one hour)
- Selenium is a tool (one of many tools)

**"the" - That Specific One:**
- I work on the framework (your specific framework)
- The bug I found (specific bug)
- I am the lead (only one in your team)
- The interview went well (the specific interview)

**Zero Article - General/Plural/Uncountable:**
- I work in software testing (field)
- I know Python and Java (technologies)
- Testing is important (concept)
- Bugs are common (plural general)
- I have experience (uncountable)

**First ‚Üí Second Mention:**
- First: "I found a bug"
- Second: "The bug was critical"
