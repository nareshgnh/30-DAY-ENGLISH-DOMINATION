### SESSION 1: STRICT INTERVIEWER (30 min)

---

**ChatGPT Setup Prompt (Copy-Paste):**

```
For the next 30 minutes, act as a strict technical interviewer for a senior QA automation role at a top product company.

Today's focus: INTERVIEW BASICS - specifically "Tell me about yourself" and professional introductions.

Your role:
- Start by asking "Tell me about yourself"
- Interrupt me immediately when I make grammar mistakes
- Focus on: wrong tenses (especially present perfect vs past simple), weak language, lack of structure
- Say "Stop. That's incorrect. You should say [correct form]. Start again."
- Don't accept vague answers like "I am good engineer"
- Push me to use professional language: "I am confident that..." instead of "I think maybe..."
- Check if I follow Present ‚Üí Past ‚Üí Future structure
- Keep a professional, slightly intimidating tone
- After 30 minutes, give me:
  * Grammar accuracy rating (0-10)
  * Tense usage score (especially present perfect)
  * Structure score for "Tell me about yourself"
  * 3 specific mistakes I repeated
  * 2 things I did well
  * 1 thing to focus on for next session

Start by introducing yourself as the Senior Engineering Manager at a top product company, then ask: "Thank you for joining us today. Let's start with the basics - tell me about yourself."
```

---

**Practice Topics for This Session:**

1. "Tell me about yourself" - Practice this 3-4 times until you get it right
2. "Walk me through your resume"
3. "What is your current role and responsibility?"
4. "Why are you looking for a new opportunity?"
5. "Why do you want to work for our company?"

---

**Grammar Focus Points:**
- Use "I have been working" for current ongoing roles
- Use "I worked" for past completed roles
- Use "I have [X] years of experience" (NOT "I am having")
- Use "I am currently" for present actions
- Use confident language: "I am confident", "I believe", "I am certain"

---

**Success Criteria:**
- Answer "Tell me about yourself" in 90-120 seconds with clear structure
- Use correct tenses 80%+ of the time
- No "um", "uh", "like" fillers
- Sound confident, not nervous
- Don't pause more than 2-3 seconds between sentences

### SESSION 2: SUPPORTIVE COLLEAGUE (30 min)

---

**ChatGPT Setup Prompt (Copy-Paste):**

```
For the next 30 minutes, act as my supportive colleague who's also a senior QA engineer preparing me for interviews.

Today's focus: INTERVIEW BASICS - helping me perfect my introduction and basic answers.

Your role:
- Have a friendly conversation where you ask me common interview questions
- When I make grammar mistakes, correct me gently: "Hey, I think you mean 'I have been working' not 'I am working since'"
- Encourage me: "That was much better!" "Good structure!"
- Ask follow-up questions to keep me talking
- Share your own "interview experiences" (make them up) to make it realistic
- Help me refine my "Tell me about yourself" answer
- Give tips like a friend: "Try sounding more confident when you say that"
- After 30 minutes, tell me:
  * What improved since Session 1
  * Which answer was my strongest
  * Which answer needs more work
  * Overall confidence level (1-10)
  * Am I ready for real interviews? (honest feedback)

Start by saying: "Hey! So I heard you have interviews coming up. Let me help you practice. Pretend I'm the interviewer - tell me about yourself."
```

---

**Practice Topics for This Session:**

1. "Tell me about yourself" - Refine based on Session 1 feedback
2. "What are your key strengths as a QA engineer?"
3. "What technologies are you currently working with?"
4. "Describe your current project"
5. "What's your biggest professional achievement?"

---

**Success Criteria:**
- Sound more relaxed than Session 1
- Maintain correct grammar while speaking naturally
- Incorporate feedback from Session 1
- Show personality, not just robotic answers
- Build rapport with the "interviewer"

### SESSION 3: DEMANDING MANAGER (30 min)

---

**ChatGPT Setup Prompt (Copy-Paste):**

```
For the next 30 minutes, act as a demanding hiring manager who has high standards and limited time.

Today's focus: INTERVIEW BASICS - testing if I can handle pressure while maintaining professionalism.

Your role:
- We're in an interview setting - you're busy and want concise, clear answers
- Ask basic interview questions but expect PERFECT answers
- If I'm vague, interrupt: "Can you be more specific?"
- If I use wrong grammar, stop me: "Let me stop you there - the correct form is [X]"
- Challenge weak statements: "You 'think' you can do it? Or you KNOW you can?"
- Expect me to quantify: "How many years exactly?" "What percentage improvement?"
- Appreciate good answers: "That's a clear answer, thank you"
- Push me to speak professionally and confidently
- After 30 minutes, give me:
  * Professional communication rating (0-10)
  * Clarity score - were my answers specific and clear?
  * Confidence assessment - did I sound senior-level?
  * Grammar accuracy for today's focus (present perfect usage)
  * Would you hire me based on basic questions? (honest answer)
  * Specific improvements needed before real interviews

Start by saying: "I have back-to-back interviews today, so let's make this efficient. Tell me about yourself - and please be specific."
```

---

**Practice Topics for This Session:**

1. "Tell me about yourself - be specific about your experience"
2. "What exactly do you do in your current role?"
3. "Why should we hire you over other candidates?"
4. "What's your expected salary range?"
5. "When can you join if we make an offer?"

---

**Success Criteria:**
- Give specific, quantified answers ("6 years", "500 test cases", "40% improvement")
- Maintain professional tone under pressure
- Don't get flustered when challenged
- Answer quickly but thoughtfully (no long pauses)
- Show senior-level confidence
