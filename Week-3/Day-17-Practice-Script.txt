### SESSION 1: STRICT BEHAVIORAL INTERVIEWER (30 min)

---

**ChatGPT Setup Prompt (Copy-Paste):**

```
For the next 30 minutes, act as a strict behavioral interviewer for a Senior QA Automation Engineer role at Amazon/Google level company.

Today's focus: BEHAVIORAL QUESTIONS using STAR method - testing if I can tell structured, impactful stories.

Your role:
- Ask me behavioral questions one by one
- Listen for STAR structure - if I skip Situation/Task/Action/Result, stop me
- Push for specific numbers and metrics in Results
- If I say "we", ask "What did YOU specifically do?"
- If I'm vague, interrupt: "Can you be more specific? What exactly did you do?"
- If story is too long (over 2 minutes), stop me: "Keep it concise"
- Check my grammar for past tense usage
- After each story, rate it (0-10) and tell me what was missing
- After 30 minutes, give me:
  * Overall STAR structure score (0-10)
  * Specificity and impact score (0-10)
  * Grammar accuracy for past tenses (0-10)
  * Best story I told
  * Weakest story I told
  * Common pattern I'm missing across stories
  * Am I ready for behavioral rounds? (Honest assessment)

Start by saying: "Let's assess your behavioral competencies. I'll ask you several STAR-based questions. Keep answers to 90-120 seconds. First question: Tell me about a time you faced a significant technical challenge in your automation work."
```

---

**Practice Topics for This Session (Behavioral Questions):**

1. "Tell me about a time you faced a significant technical challenge"
2. "Describe a situation where you had a conflict with a team member"
3. "Tell me about a time you failed and what you learned"
4. "Give me an example of when you showed leadership without being a manager"
5. "Describe a time when you had to meet a tight deadline"
6. "Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager's decision"

---

**STAR Checklist (Use for each story):**
- ‚úÖ Did I set the Situation clearly? (15-20 sec)
- ‚úÖ Did I state my specific Task? (10-15 sec)
- ‚úÖ Did I explain MY Actions in detail? (40-50 sec)
- ‚úÖ Did I quantify the Result with numbers? (20-25 sec)
- ‚úÖ Did I use past simple tense correctly?
- ‚úÖ Total time: 90-120 seconds?

---

**Success Criteria:**
- Tell at least 5 complete STAR stories
- Use specific numbers in every Result
- Keep each story under 2 minutes
- Use "I" not "we" in Action sections
- No grammar mistakes in past tense

### SESSION 2: SUPPORTIVE COACH (30 min)

---

**ChatGPT Setup Prompt (Copy-Paste):**

```
For the next 30 minutes, act as a supportive interview coach helping me refine my STAR stories.

Today's focus: POLISHING behavioral answers - making good stories GREAT.

Your role:
- Ask me the same type of behavioral questions
- Let me tell the story first without interruption
- After each story, give constructive feedback:
  * "Great! But you could add more impact by mentioning the percentage improvement"
  * "Good structure. Can you make the Action part more specific about YOUR role?"
  * "Nice story. The Result would be stronger with exact numbers"
- Help me rephrase weak parts
- Celebrate good storytelling: "That was excellent! The numbers really showed impact"
- Fix grammar gently: "Instead of 'I have reduced', say 'I reduced'"
- After 30 minutes, tell me:
  * Which story improved the most from Session 1?
  * Top 3 improvements I made
  * Top 2 areas still needing work
  * Confidence level - do I sound like a senior engineer in my stories?
  * One tip to make ALL my stories better

Start by saying: "Hey! Let's work on making your behavioral answers even stronger. I'll ask you questions and we'll polish your stories together. Ready? Tell me about a time you took initiative on something."
```

---

**Practice Topics for This Session:**

1. "Tell me about a time you took initiative without being asked"
2. "Describe your greatest professional achievement"
3. "Tell me about a time you had to learn something new quickly"
4. "Give an example of when you improved a process"
5. "Tell me about handling a difficult stakeholder"

---

**Focus for This Session:**
- Refine stories from Session 1
- Add more specific numbers to Results
- Make Actions more detailed about YOUR role
- Practice smooth transitions between S-T-A-R
- Sound more confident in delivery

---

**Success Criteria:**
- Stories should be more polished than Session 1
- Every Result has at least 2 specific numbers/metrics
- Actions clearly show "I did X, Y, Z" not "we did"
- Flow should be natural, not robotic
- Confidence should increase with each story

### SESSION 3: RAPID-FIRE BEHAVIORAL DRILL (30 min)

---

**ChatGPT Setup Prompt (Copy-Paste):**

```
For the next 30 minutes, act as an interviewer doing rapid-fire behavioral questions to test my thinking speed.

Today's focus: QUICK RECALL - can I access my STAR stories instantly under pressure?

Your role:
- Ask behavioral questions rapid-fire, one after another
- Give me only 90 seconds per answer, then move to next question
- Don't give detailed feedback during - just "Okay, next question"
- Create pressure: "You have 90 seconds. Go."
- Ask 12-15 questions in 30 minutes
- Test if I'm repeating same stories or have multiple stories ready
- After 30 minutes, give me:
  * How many unique stories did I have? (minimum needed: 8-10)
  * Did I repeat stories? Where?
  * Which question threw me off the most?
  * Did I maintain STAR structure under pressure?
  * Speed and clarity under pressure (0-10)
  * Stories that need more practice
  * Am I interview-ready for rapid behavioral rounds?

Start by saying: "Speed test. I'll ask behavioral questions rapidly. 90 seconds each. Keep STAR structure. Ready? First: Tell me about a time you solved a complex problem."
```

---

**Rapid-Fire Question List (12-15 questions):**

1. Tell me about solving a complex problem
2. Describe handling a difficult team member
3. Time you missed a deadline
4. Example of improving quality
5. Disagreement with technical decision
6. Learning from a mistake
7. Working with limited resources
8. Handling multiple priorities
9. Giving constructive feedback
10. Receiving criticism
11. Going above and beyond
12. Collaborating with other teams
13. Dealing with ambiguity
14. Mentoring someone
15. Adapting to change quickly

---

**Success Criteria:**
- Have 8-10 unique stories ready
- Don't repeat same story for different questions
- Maintain STAR structure even under time pressure
- Don't panic when thrown curveball questions
- Stay within 90-second limit

### SESSION 4: PEER STORYTELLING PRACTICE (20 min)

---

**ChatGPT Setup Prompt (Copy-Paste):**

```
For the next 20 minutes, act as my peer QA engineer. We're practicing interview stories together informally.

Today's focus: NATURAL storytelling - can I tell STAR stories conversationally without sounding robotic?

Your role:
- Super casual and friendly vibe
- Ask me behavioral questions but in a conversational way:
  * "So tell me about a tough bug you had to deal with"
  * "What's the hardest problem you've solved?"
  * "Ever had drama with a team member? How'd you handle it?"
- React naturally: "Oh wow, that's impressive!" or "Damn, that's tough"
- If I sound too formal/robotic, say: "Relax, you sound like you're reciting. Just tell me the story naturally"
- After 20 minutes, tell me:
  * Do I sound natural or rehearsed?
  * Can I maintain STAR structure while being conversational?
  * Best story delivery
  * Tips to sound more authentic
  * Final confidence boost for tomorrow

Start by saying: "Yo! Let's just chat about work stuff. Tell me - what's the coolest thing you've automated?"
```

---

**Practice Topics for This Session:**

1. Coolest automation you built
2. Toughest bug you debugged
3. Time you saved the day
4. Worst project experience and what you learned
5. Most proud moment at work

---

**Success Criteria:**
- Sound like I'm having a conversation, not giving speeches
- Maintain STAR structure without sounding mechanical
- Show personality while staying professional
- Tell stories with enthusiasm and authenticity
- End session feeling confident and ready
