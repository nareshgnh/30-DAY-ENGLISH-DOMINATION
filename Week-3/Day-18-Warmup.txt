**BREATHING EXERCISE (2 min):**

1. **Natural Conversation Breath:**
   - Breathe in for 4 counts: "I can talk naturally about anything"
   - Hold for 4 counts: "Conversations flow easily for me"
   - Breathe out for 6 counts: "I am relaxed and authentic"
   - Repeat 3 times

2. **Rapport Building Breath:**
   - Breathe in deeply: "People enjoy talking with me"
   - Breathe out slowly: "I make genuine connections"
   - Repeat 3 times

3. **Authenticity Breath:**
   - Breathe in: "I don't need to be perfect"
   - Breathe out: "I just need to be myself"
   - Final breath: "Real and professional"
   - Repeat 3 times

---

**WARM-UP SENTENCES (5 min):**

Read each sentence aloud 3 times. These are natural small talk sentences.

1. "How was your weekend? Did you get a chance to relax?"

2. "The weather has been unpredictable lately, hasn't it?"

3. "I've been really into cycling recently. Do you have any hobbies outside of work?"

4. "I saw that news about the new AI developments. Pretty exciting times for tech!"

5. "Thanks for taking the time to meet with me today. I really appreciate it."

---

**TONGUE TWISTERS (3 min):**

Practice these 5 times each for conversational fluency:

1. "We were wondering whether the weather would work well for the weekend workshop."

2. "I recently read reviews regarding really remarkable restaurants recommended by residents."

3. "Please share your perspective on the project's progress and potential plans."

**QUICK GRAMMAR RECALL (5 min):**

**Yesterday's Review (Day 17):**
- You mastered STAR method for behavioral questions
- You learned to use past simple for storytelling
- You practiced quantifying results with numbers

**Today's Preview (Day 18 - Workplace Small Talk):**
- Focus: Present Perfect for recent activities ("Have you watched...", "Have you been to...")
- Focus: Question tags for friendly tone ("Nice weather, isn't it?")
- Focus: Conversational connectors ("Actually...", "By the way...")
- Focus: Active listening and follow-up questions

**Key Grammar for Today:**
- "How was your weekend?" (past simple for completed weekend)
- "Have you watched [show]?" (present perfect for recent/relevant experience)
- "Nice weather, isn't it?" (question tag for friendliness)
- "I've been meaning to try that" (present perfect continuous for intentions)
