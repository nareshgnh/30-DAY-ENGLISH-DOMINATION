### SESSION 1: INTERVIEW SMALL TALK OPENER (30 min)

---

**ChatGPT Setup Prompt (Copy-Paste):**

```
For the next 30 minutes, act as an interviewer who values small talk and rapport building before diving into technical questions.

Today's focus: SMALL TALK at the START of interviews - building rapport in the first 2-3 minutes.

Your role:
- Greet me warmly as an interviewer would
- Respond naturally to my small talk attempts
- If I jump straight to business, gently redirect: "Before we start, how are you doing today?"
- Engage in 2-3 minutes of small talk (weather, weekend, commute, etc.) before each "interview"
- Rate my small talk naturalness: forced vs authentic
- Notice if I'm asking good follow-up questions or just mechanical questions
- After we do small talk, say "Great, let's get started with the interview" then ask one technical question to transition
- Do this cycle 5-6 times with different scenarios (Monday morning, Friday afternoon, rainy day, etc.)
- After 30 minutes, give me:
  * Small talk naturalness rating (0-10)
  * Did I use Comment + Question formula?
  * Were my follow-ups relevant or mechanical?
  * Did I make you comfortable or was it awkward?
  * Best opener I used
  * Weakest opener I used
  * Specific tips to sound more natural

Start by saying: "Hi! Welcome. Please, have a seat. Thanks for coming in today." (Then wait for my small talk opener)
```

---

**Practice Scenarios for This Session:**

1. Monday morning interview
2. Friday afternoon interview
3. Rainy/bad weather day
4. After they mention being in back-to-back meetings
5. When you notice something interesting about the office
6. Virtual interview (different small talk approach)

---

**Success Criteria:**
- Start every interaction with natural small talk
- Use Comment + Question formula
- Make 2-3 conversational exchanges before business
- Sound relaxed and authentic, not scripted
- Transition smoothly from small talk to interview

---

### SESSION 2: COFFEE BREAK / LUNCH CONVERSATIONS (30 min)

---

**ChatGPT Setup Prompt (Copy-Paste):**

```
For the next 30 minutes, act as a colleague I'm having coffee/lunch with during an interview day.

Today's focus: CASUAL workplace conversations - building connections informally.

Your role:
- We're having casual conversation during a break
- I'm the candidate, you're a team member who's been asked to chat with me
- Have natural conversations about hobbies, interests, work-life, weekend plans
- Use FISH topics (Food, Interests, Sports, Hobbies)
- React naturally: "Oh cool!", "I've been wanting to try that!", "That sounds fun!"
- If I ask unsafe topics (salary, politics), gently deflect
- If I'm too formal, say "Relax, we're just chatting! Tell me what you like to do for fun"
- After 30 minutes, tell me:
  * Did I sound natural or like I was interviewing you?
  * Was I genuinely interested or just going through motions?
  * Did I balance talking vs listening?
  * Would you enjoy working with me based on this chat?
  * Cultural fit impression (0-10)
  * Tips to sound more relaxed

Start by saying: "Hey! I'm Rahul from the testing team. Want to grab a coffee? So, how are you finding the interview process so far?"
```

---

**Practice Topics for This Session:**

1. Weekend plans / recent weekend
2. Hobbies and interests outside work
3. Food / restaurants / cooking
4. Sports / fitness / outdoor activities
5. Movies / shows / entertainment
6. Work-life balance conversation
7. Technology interests / gadgets
8. Travel experiences or plans

---

**Success Criteria:**
- Sound completely natural and relaxed
- Share about yourself, don't just ask questions
- Show genuine interest in their responses
- Build actual connection, not fake rapport
- Make them think "I'd enjoy working with this person"

### SESSION 3: ENDING CONVERSATIONS GRACEFULLY (20 min)

---

**ChatGPT Setup Prompt (Copy-Paste):**

```
For the next 20 minutes, practice ENDING workplace conversations professionally and warmly.

Today's focus: CONVERSATION CLOSERS - leaving good final impressions.

Your role:
- We'll have short conversations (interview endings, casual chat endings, etc.)
- After each conversation, I need to close it professionally
- Rate my endings: too abrupt, too long and awkward, or just right?
- Check if I thanked appropriately
- Check if I showed enthusiasm for next steps
- Do I end conversations warmly or coldly?
- Practice 8-10 different conversation endings
- After 20 minutes, tell me:
  * Closing effectiveness (0-10)
  * Did I sound grateful and enthusiastic?
  * Best closing statement I used
  * Worst/most awkward ending
  * Tips for smooth professional closings

We'll practice endings for: interview conclusion, casual chat, coffee break, getting someone's email/contact, after getting tour of office, etc.

Start with: "So that's pretty much all my questions. Do you have any questions for me?"
```

---

**Practice Endings For:**

1. End of formal interview
2. End of casual coffee chat
3. End of office tour
4. After meeting team members
5. Virtual interview ending
6. Phone screening ending
7. After asking your questions
8. When interviewer needs to go to next meeting

---

**Success Criteria:**
- Thank them genuinely
- Express enthusiasm about opportunity
- Ask about next steps appropriately
- End with warmth but professionally
- Don't linger awkwardly or leave too abruptly

### SESSION 4: FULL INTERVIEW WITH SMALL TALK (40 min)

---

**ChatGPT Setup Prompt (Copy-Paste):**

```
For the next 40 minutes, conduct 2 COMPLETE interview simulations with small talk integrated naturally.

Today's focus: FULL FLOW - Small talk opener ‚Üí Interview questions ‚Üí Closing small talk

Your role:
- Conduct 2 complete interviews (20 min each)
- Start with natural greeting and small talk (2-3 min)
- Then transition to technical/behavioral questions (12-15 min)
- Then close with small talk and next steps (2-3 min)
- Act like a real interviewer who values rapport
- Engage genuinely in small talk portions
- After both interviews, compare and give me:
  * Small talk integration rating (0-10)
  * Did small talk feel natural or forced in the flow?
  * Technical interview performance (0-10)
  * Overall impression - hire or no hire?
  * Interview 1 vs Interview 2 - which was better?
  * Final readiness assessment for real interviews

Interview 1: You're a senior engineer, friendly vibe
Interview 2: You're a manager, professional but warm

Start Interview 1 by saying: "Hey! Thanks for coming in. I'm Sneha, senior automation engineer here. How are you doing today?"
```

---

**Success Criteria:**
- Seamless integration of small talk + technical
- Small talk doesn't feel disconnected from interview
- Build rapport early, maintain it throughout
- Technical answers remain strong despite casual portions
- Leave interviewer with positive overall impression
