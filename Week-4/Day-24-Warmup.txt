## BREATHING EXERCISE (2 min):

**Exercise 1: Power & Conviction Breath (30 seconds)**
- Stand tall, shoulders back
- Inhale deeply for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Exhale powerfully while saying: "I bring immense value and they need what I offer."
- Repeat 3 times with absolute conviction

**Exercise 2: Negotiation Confidence Breath (30 seconds)**
- Breathe normally
- With each breath, say with confidence:
  * "I deserve 25 LPA"
  * "My skills are valuable"
  * "I will negotiate with confidence"

**Exercise 3: Influential Speaker Breath (1 minute)**
- Take 3 deep breaths
- Say these persuasive statements powerfully:
  * "The reason you should hire me is clear: I deliver results."
  * "This will benefit your organization in three significant ways."
  * "Consider what I bring to the table: expertise, experience, and proven impact."

## WARM-UP SENTENCES (5 min):

**Read these aloud 3 times, feeling the persuasive power:**

1. "The reason you should consider me for this role is straightforward: I've successfully built automation frameworks that delivered 300% ROI within six months. That's not a promise - that's my track record."

2. "Let me show you why investing in comprehensive automation will transform your testing process. First, you'll reduce costs by 60%. Second, you'll accelerate releases by weeks. Third, you'll improve quality dramatically. This isn't optional anymore - it's essential."

3. "I'm not just looking for any role - I'm looking for the right partnership. What I bring is 6 years of proven expertise. What I need is an organization that values quality and innovation. That's why this role interests me."

4. "Consider this perspective: hiring me isn't an expense - it's an investment that will pay for itself in the first quarter through efficiency gains and quality improvements."

5. "I'd like to propose a salary range of 20-25 LPA based on three factors: my 6 years of specialized experience, the measurable impact I've delivered in previous roles, and the market value for senior automation engineers with my skill set."
