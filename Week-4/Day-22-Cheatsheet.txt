# ‚ö° TOP 5 GRAMMAR RULES

---

## Rule 1: TYPE 2 CONDITIONAL (Present/Future Hypothetical)

**Pattern:** If + were/past simple, would/could + base verb

‚úÖ "If I were the tech lead, I would change the architecture."
‚úÖ "If we had more resources, we could implement parallel testing."

---

## Rule 2: TYPE 3 CONDITIONAL (Past Hypothetical)

**Pattern:** If + had + past participle, would have + past participle

‚úÖ "If I had known about Playwright earlier, I would have migrated the framework."
‚úÖ "If we had started automation sooner, we would have saved significant time."

---

## Rule 3: PAST PERFECT CONTINUOUS (Duration Before Past)

**Pattern:** had been + verb(-ing)

‚úÖ "I had been working on manual testing for 5 years before I switched to automation."
‚úÖ "The team had been struggling with flaky tests before I implemented proper waits."

---

## Rule 4: FUTURE PERFECT (Completion Before Future Time)

**Pattern:** will have + past participle

‚úÖ "By the end of this month, I will have completed the framework migration."
‚úÖ "By Day 30, I will have practiced English for over 70 hours."

---

## Rule 5: MIXED CONDITIONAL (Past Cause, Present Effect)

**Pattern:** If + had + past participle, would + base verb

‚úÖ "If I had learned English earlier, I would be more confident now."
‚úÖ "If we had invested in automation from the start, we wouldn't have these issues today."

---

# ‚ùå TOP 5 MISTAKES TO AVOID

---

1. ‚ùå "If I was the manager..."
   ‚úÖ "If I were the manager..."
   **Always use "were" for all subjects in Type 2 conditionals!**

2. ‚ùå "If I would have known..."
   ‚úÖ "If I had known..."
   **Never use "would" in the "if" clause for Type 3!**

3. ‚ùå "By next month, I will complete..."
   ‚úÖ "By next month, I will have completed..."
   **Use Future Perfect with "by + future time"!**

4. ‚ùå "I was working for 3 years before I switched..."
   ‚úÖ "I had been working for 3 years before I switched..."
   **Use Past Perfect Continuous to show duration before past event!**

5. ‚ùå "If I learned English earlier, I would be confident now."
   ‚úÖ "If I had learned English earlier, I would be more confident now."
   **Mixed conditional needs "had" for the past part!**
