# COMPLETE MOCK INTERVIEW STRUCTURE

## PHASE 1: PREPARATION (30 min before interview)

**Setup:**
1. Choose quiet location with good internet
2. Dress professionally (yes, even for ChatGPT practice!)
3. Have resume ready
4. Prepare notebook for notes
5. Set up recording (voice/video if possible)
6. Do 5 rounds of 4-7-8 breathing
7. Review your key achievements and talking points
8. Get into interview mindset

**Mental Preparation:**
- This is a REAL interview
- You will be evaluated
- Record everything for analysis
- Give 100% effort

---

## PHASE 2: THE INTERVIEW (120 minutes)

**Section 1: Introduction & Warm-up (15 min)**

Expected Questions:
- "Tell me about yourself"
- "Walk me through your resume"
- "Why are you interested in this role?"
- "What do you know about our company?"

What You're Evaluated On:
- Clear structure (Hook-Body-Conclusion)
- Industry vocabulary usage
- Confidence level
- Grammar accuracy
- Engagement

---

**Section 2: Technical Deep Dive (40 min)**

Expected Questions:
- "Walk me through your automation framework"
- "Explain your architecture decisions"
- "What tools and technologies do you use?"
- "How do you handle flaky tests?"
- "How is your framework integrated with CI/CD?"
- "What's your approach to test data management?"
- "How do you measure test coverage?"

What You're Evaluated On:
- Technical depth
- Presentation skills (signposting, structure)
- Industry terminology
- Clarity of explanation
- Confidence under technical pressure

---

**Section 3: Behavioral Questions (30 min)**

Expected Questions:
- "Tell me about a challenging bug you solved"
- "Describe a time you disagreed with a team member"
- "Tell me about a project that failed"
- "How do you prioritize when you have multiple deadlines?"
- "Describe a time you led an initiative"

What You're Evaluated On:
- STAR method usage
- Storytelling quality
- Advanced grammar (conditionals, perfect tenses)
- Reflection and learning demonstrated
- Professional maturity

---

**Section 4: Pressure Questions (15 min)**

Expected Questions:
- "Why are you leaving your current company?"
- "Why should we hire you over other candidates?"
- "What's your biggest weakness?"
- "You seem to lack experience in [X]"
- "Your experience seems too service-company focused"

What You're Evaluated On:
- Staying calm under pressure
- Persuasive language
- Turning negatives to positives
- Confidence (not defensiveness)
- Pause-Think-Speak pattern

---

**Section 5: Salary Negotiation (10 min)**

Expected Questions:
- "What are your salary expectations?"
- "That seems high for your experience level"
- "We typically offer X range, which is below your expectation"

What You're Evaluated On:
- Negotiation language
- Confidence in stating expectations
- Justification with data/value
- Flexibility while maintaining position
- Professional demeanor

---

**Section 6: Your Questions (10 min)**

Your Opportunity to Ask:
- About team structure
- About testing processes
- About growth opportunities
- About tech stack
- About company culture
- About next steps

What You're Evaluated On:
- Quality of questions (show research/interest)
- Professional curiosity
- Strategic thinking
- Communication clarity

---
